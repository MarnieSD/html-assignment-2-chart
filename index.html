Reports of Hate Incidents Have Risen in NYC Over the Past Two Years

Complaints and arrests related to hate incidents are a growing issue in New York, and there is no singular reason or solution for it.

By Marnie Dunbar

Dec. 5, 2022

Beginning in 2021, New York City saw a noticeable increase in the number of hate-related incidents.

“I’d say there are a number of factors that are contributing to the rise in hate-related incidents,” says Sherri Deckman, Professor of Social Welfare at Lehman College.

“These include the open embrace of hate and violence by elected officials, which is not necessarily anything new. However, there has been increased vitriol enacted at the highest levels of institutional power in recent history. Additionally, the conditions of the pandemic have likely provided a fertile breeding ground of fear and suspicion.”

On the charts below, hate incidents have been categorized by quarters, with each quarter representing three months of the year. It is also worth noting that this most recent quarter (July-September 2022) has the highest percentage of complaints to arrests at 67%. This is the first time in several years that it has been over 50%.
